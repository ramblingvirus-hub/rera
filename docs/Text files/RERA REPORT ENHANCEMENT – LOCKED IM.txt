## SECTION 2 — REQUIRED REPORT STRUCTURE

All reports MUST include the following sections:

1. Assessment Summary (ALWAYS PRESENT)
2. Category Breakdown with Interpretation
3. Strength Indicators (NEW)
4. Key Risk Signals (if any)
5. Information Gaps & Unverified Claims
6. Recommended Next Steps (ALWAYS PRESENT)

## SECTION 3 — ASSESSMENT SUMMARY

Template:

"This project appears to present a [LOW / MODERATE / HIGH] level of risk based on the information provided.

Key strengths include:
• {auto-generated strengths}

Potential concerns include:
• {only if exists}

While no major red flags are identified, buyers are still encouraged to verify all documents independently before proceeding."

Rules:

* Always generated
* Must include at least 1 strength
* Concerns section optional if none

## SECTION 4 — STRENGTH INDICATORS (NEW)

Purpose:
Highlight positive signals from user responses

Examples:

* Developer has presented a valid License to Sell
* Development permit appears secured
* No known title disputes reported
* No environmental risks indicated

Rules:

* Derived from positive answers (YES / CLEAN)
* Minimum 2 items
* If none: "No major risk indicators detected"

---

## SECTION 5 — CATEGORY INTERPRETATION

Each category must include:

* Score
* Label
* 1-sentence explanation

Labels:
80–100 → Strong
60–79 → Moderate
40–59 → Weak
0–39 → High Risk

Example:

"Developer Legitimacy — Strong (85/100)
The developer appears established and has provided key regulatory documentation."

---

## SECTION 6 — INFORMATION GAPS & UNVERIFIED CLAIMS

Two types:

A. Information Gaps
Triggered by "Not Sure"

Examples:

* Environmental risks are unclear
* Title condition not confirmed

B. Unverified Claims
Triggered by responses like:

* "No documents shown"
* "Seller claims but no proof"
* "Developer says not required"

Examples:

* Seller claims title is clean but no documents were shown
* Developer indicates ECC is not required without verification

Rules:

* At least 1 item must be present
* If none: "No major information gaps identified"

---

## SECTION 7 — RECOMMENDED NEXT STEPS

Purpose:
Make report actionable regardless of risk level

Examples:

* Verify License to Sell with DHSUD
* Request certified true copy of the title
* Confirm permits with local government
* Conduct a physical site visit

Rules:

* ALWAYS present
* Minimum 3 items
* Must include at least 1 regulatory check

## SECTION 10 — HELPER CONTENT

Q7 — License to Sell

What it is:
Issued by DHSUD, this allows a developer to legally sell units.

Why it matters:
Selling without this may indicate unauthorized or premature selling.

Impact on assessment:
YES → Strong positive signal
NO → High-risk trigger
Not Sure → Information gap

Tip: If unsure, select “Not Sure” — this will highlight areas you may need to verify.

---

Q9 — Development Permit

What it is:
Approval from the local government to begin development.

Why it matters:
Without this, construction may be illegal or halted.

Impact on assessment:
YES → Improves compliance score
NO → Major regulatory risk
Not Sure → Weakens confidence

Tip: If unsure, select “Not Sure” — this will highlight areas you may need to verify.

---

Q10 — Environmental Compliance Certificate (ECC)

What it is:
Environmental clearance required for certain developments.

Why it matters:
Projects without proper clearance may face stoppage or penalties.

Impact on assessment:
YES → Positive signal
NO → Environmental/legal risk
"Not required" → Unverified claim

Tip: If unsure, select “Not Sure” — this will highlight areas you may need to verify.

---

Q11 — Title Type

What it is:
Indicates the form of land ownership.

Why it matters:
Some title types carry higher legal risk.

Impact on assessment:
TCT/CCT → Strong
Mother Title → Moderate caution
Rights Only / No title → High risk

Tip: If unsure, select “Not Sure” — this will highlight areas you may need to verify.

---

Q12 — Title Issues

What it is:
Indicates if there are disputes or claims on the property.

Why it matters:
Disputes can delay or prevent ownership transfer.

Impact on assessment:

Tip: If unsure, select “Not Sure” — this will highlight areas you may need to verify.
